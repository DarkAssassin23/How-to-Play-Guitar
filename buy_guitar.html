Buying a Guitar

When it comes to buying a guitar, there are several things to keep in mind:

  1. What kind of music will you be playing?
  2. What kind of sound are you looking for?
  3. What is your budget?
    • Are you willing to spend money on other equitment? (amps, cables, power cords etc.)

If you're on a tight budget, or don't have extra money to buy extra accessories, you'll want to go with an acoustic or a classical (A.K.A Spanish Guitar). However, acoustic would most likely be the cheepest option, depending on what brand you buy.

Similarly, if you want more of a classical guitar sound, you'll want to go with an acoustic or classical guitar. The main difference between a classical and acoustic guitar is the strings and how they are wound. A regualr acoustic guitar uses steel strings and can be strumed with a pick, and also has a solid head where the strings wrap around the tuning pegs. A classical, on the other hand, uses nylon strings and should only be played with your fingers. The classical also has an open head unlike the solid head of the acoustic.

However, if you have more money to spend, and or want more of a rock sound and be able to play around with various effects with your guitar, then electric is the guitar for you. Keep in mind, that in order to get the full experience out of your electric guitar you'll need to make sure you get an amp and a cable to connect your guitar to the amp at a minimum.
